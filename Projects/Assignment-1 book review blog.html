Kingdom of the Cursed

10/06/2024 | 24 min | Kerri Maniscalco



                  

Introduction:

“One thing I can promise,” his voice was deep and sensual in the dark, “is you won’t ever doubt it’s real when I touch you.” Frustrated, embarrassed, and furious to have submitted to desiring him for even one second, I plucked up his shirt and roughly tugged it back on before flopping onto my side. “Someone’s cocky.” “Says the person rubbing up against my c—”

Summary:

With the enigmatic Prince of Wrath at her side, Emilia sold her soul to become Queen of the Wicked and travelled to the Seven Circles to fulfil her vow of avenging her beloved sister. But the first rule in the court of the Wicked? Trust no one. And it quickly becomes clear that nothing in Hell is what it seems.

Review:

3.8/5 Goodreads | 4/5 the book blog life

I loved Kingdom of the Wicked, the first in the trilogy by Kerri Maniscalco, but I loooooved Kingdom of the Cursed! The book picks up where Kingdom of the Wicked left off. Emilia has decided to enter a deal with Pride so she can hopefully find out who was behind the murder of her sister, Vittoria.

Additional Thoughts:

Well this didn't fully live up to book one. Perhaps my expectation was to high or perhaps it just felt bit more of the same. Now don't get me wrong, I liked how it dived more into each Prince of Hell and their story. I liked you got to see more of the kingdom and realm they rule over and their laws and politically correct standards. What I don't like is how it felt it was just all one big lead up to Kingdom of the Feared.

Conclusion:

“Kingdom of the Cursed final chapters were the best part of the book. It gave me hope for an epic conclusion for the final book to this series.” 

-Sheaf & Ink


Song of Silver, Flame Like Night

12/06/2024 | 32 min | Amélie Wen Zhao



                 

Introduction:

In a fallen kingdom, one girl carries the key to discovering the secrets of her nation's past—and unleashing the demons that sleep at its heart. An epic fantasy series inspired by the mythology and folklore of ancient China.

Summary:

A Song of Silver, Flame Like Night follows two main characters. Lan lives under the rule of the Elantian colonizers who invaded her kingdom and killed her mother.

Review:

4/5 Goodreads | 4.2/5 the book blog life

It is the start of a new series by Amélie Wen Zhao. An epic story in the crossover territory between YA and adult fantasy, strongly influenced by Zhao’s Chinese background and multicultural upbringing and full of immersive prose, this will whisk you away and immerse you completely.

Additional Thoughts:

Lan had a different name, but now she goes by the one the Elantian colonizers gave her. She spends her days scavenging for remnants of the past. For anything that might help her understand the strange mark burned into her arm by her mother, in her last act before she died.

Conclusion:

It is a captivating and immersive read that will transport you to a richly detailed world inspired by Chinese culture and mythology. The story is full of action, magic, and intrigue, with complex characters and morally ambiguous themes that will keep you guessing until the very end.

27/06/2024 | 47 min | Naomi Novik



                 

Introduction:

author of book the forest of dragons artistic binding

Summary:

The story follows Princess Cimorene, who is bored with her life and decides to volunteer to be a captive princess of a dragon named Kazul. However, she soon finds herself explaining to knights and princes that she doesn't need to be rescued and is happy living with the dragon.

Review:

4/5 Goodreads | 3.8/5 the book blog life

The reviewer praises the book for its importance in challenging received wisdom and encouraging children to think for themselves. The book's message is simple yet important

Additional Thoughts:

One of the interesting aspects of the book is how it subverts traditional fairy tale tropes. Instead of running away to have adventures or save the kingdom, Cimorene runs away to get a job and live a more ordinary life.

Conclusion:

Overall, "Dealing with Dragons" is a delightful and clever book that challenges traditional fairy tale tropes and encourages children to think for themselves.


Ebony Gate: The Phoenix Hoard

29/06/2024 | 40 min | Julia Vee and Ken Bebelle



                 

Introduction:

Ebony Gate: The Phoenix Hoard is a fantasy novel by Michael Carrol, published in 2020. This book is the first installment in The Phoenix Hoard series.

Summary:

Summary The story follows Lyra, a young orphan who discovers she has the ability to wield magic. She is taken under the wing of a mysterious organization known as the Ebony Gate, which is dedicated to protecting the world from dark forces.

Review:

3.5/5 Goodreads | 3.9/5 the book blog life

I was thoroughly impressed with Carrol's world-building in Ebony Gate. The author has created a richly detailed and immersive world, filled with unique magical creatures, ancient artifacts, and hidden societies.

Additional Thoughts:

One of the things that struck me about Ebony Gate is the way it explores themes of power, identity, and belonging.

Conclusion:

Overall, I highly recommend Ebony Gate: The Phoenix Hoard to fans of fantasy and adventure stories. Carrol has created a rich and immersive world, filled with complex characters, unique magic, and ancient secrets.

Rating:

★★★★★
